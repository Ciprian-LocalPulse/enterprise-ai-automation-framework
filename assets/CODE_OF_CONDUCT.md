## Our Standard

The AI Automation Empire community — including all authorized contributors, licensed partners, practitioners, and enterprise clients — operates under an uncompromising standard of professional excellence, mutual respect, and ethical integrity.

We build enterprise-grade systems. Our conduct must be enterprise-grade too.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Professional Excellence
Every interaction — whether in code, documentation, communication, or community discussion — reflects the caliber of work we deliver to Fortune 500 clients. Mediocrity in conduct is incompatible with premium positioning.

### 2. Mutual Respect
We engage every person — regardless of experience level, background, company size, or technical skill — with the same professional respect we extend to our most valued enterprise clients. There is no hierarchy of human dignity here.

### 3. Security Consciousness
We are a security-first community. Every member understands that careless disclosure of techniques, credentials, client data, or proprietary systems creates real harm. Security awareness is not optional — it is foundational.

### 4. Radical Honesty
We communicate findings, failures, risks, and limitations with complete transparency. There is no place for misleading claims, inflated metrics, or false promises in an enterprise operation.

### 5. Confidentiality
All proprietary content, client information, system architectures, business strategies, and community communications are confidential by default. Disclosure outside authorized channels is a violation of this code and potentially of law.

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors will result in immediate removal from the community and, where applicable, legal action:

- **Harassment or intimidation** of any kind — in any channel, public or private.
- **Discrimination** based on race, gender, nationality, religion, disability, sexual orientation, or any protected characteristic.
- **Unauthorized disclosure** of proprietary content, trade secrets, client data, or confidential community communications.
- **Plagiarism or IP theft** — claiming authorship of work that is not yours, or reproducing proprietary materials without authorization.
- **Misrepresentation** — falsely claiming certifications, credentials, client results, or affiliations.
- **Security violations** — sharing credentials, exploiting vulnerabilities, or circumventing access controls.
- **Spam or self-promotion** — unsolicited promotion of competing products or services in community channels.
- **Bad faith engagement** — joining the community or repository to extract competitive intelligence without legitimate authorization.

## Enforcement

Violations are handled at the discretion of Ciprian Stefan Plesca as the sole governing authority of this community.

| Severity | Action |
|----------|--------|
| First minor offense | Private written warning with corrective guidance |
| Repeated minor offense | Temporary suspension from community and repository access |
| Major offense (harassment, IP violation, security breach) | Permanent removal, license revocation, and legal referral |
| Criminal conduct | Immediate referral to appropriate law enforcement |

There is no appeal process for permanent removals resulting from major violations.
